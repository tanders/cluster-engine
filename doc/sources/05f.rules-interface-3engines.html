rule-3-engines-metric-timepoints-events-and-pitch-include-rests-indicate-duration   simple-rule rhythm-engine2 pitch-engine3 flag-for-metric-access  [Function]

Formats a rule for how notes (i.e. durations with pitch or rests) are positioned at metric points. Grace notes are ignored. Also the length of the duration or rest will be passed to the logic statement.

The logic statement will receive '(offset duration pitch).

If the pitch information is not used: use the rule that only checks two engiens (it will be more efficient).

rule-3-engines-metric-timepoints-events-and-pitch-include-rests-indicate-duration-and-meter   simple-rule rhythm-engine2 pitch-engine3 flag-for-metric-access  [Function]

Formats a rule for how notes (i.e. durations with pitch or rests) are positioned at metric points. Grace notes are ignored. Also the length of the duration or rest will be passed to the logic statement, and the time signature at the metric time point.

The logic statement will receive '(offset duration pitch timesign).

If the pitch information is not used: use the rule that only checks two engiens (it will be more efficient).

Formats a rule for how events (i.e. durations or rests) are positioned at metric points. Grace notes are ignored. Also the length of the duration or rest will be passed to the logic statement.

The logic statement will receive a list of all metric timepoints '((offset duration pitch) (offset duration pitch) ...).

rule-three-engines1   rule engine1 engine2 engine3  [Function]

This is for meter (engine1), rhythm (engine2) and pitch (engine3) in the same voice. It prefers backtrack in the same engine. The second preference is to backtrack the rhythm engine. The third preference is to backtrack the pitch engine. The fourth preference is to backtrack the metric engine.

rule-three-engines2   rule engine1 engine2 engine3  [Function]

This is for meter (engine1), rhythm (engine2) and pitch (engine3) in the same voice. It prefers backtrack in /the other engine/, i.e. not the metric engine. The metric engine prefers to backtrack the rhythm engine (then the pitch engine). The last preference is always to backtrack the metric engine.
